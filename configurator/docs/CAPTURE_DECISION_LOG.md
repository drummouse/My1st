| D-071 | **Three hardening corrections to the PR #28 drain worker**, found in independent review: (1) `commsDelivery.js` classified every HTTP status below 500 as `permanent`, which made a `429` (rate-limited) or `408` (request timeout) response terminal on the first attempt instead of retried — both are now explicitly `transient` via a `classifyProviderStatus()` helper; every other 4xx (bad number, unverified recipient, blocked sender, etc.) stays `permanent`. Both `sendTwilioSms`/`sendGridEmail` now wrap their fetch in an `AbortController` deadline (`withProviderDeadline`), capped at `PROVIDER_TIMEOUT_MS` (5s) and further bounded by whatever's actually left of the invocation's own budget (`providerTimeoutFor`); a timed-out request classifies `transient`, and a row with too little budget left (`< MIN_ROW_BUDGET_MS`, 500ms) is released back to `pending` without ever being attempted.
